# Sprint 5 Publication Rationale (Methods-First Framing)

## Why Publish This Result
- It provides a reproducible, significance-calibrated workflow with explicit false-positive controls.
- It documents multiple detector-family attempts and their measured failure modes, reducing duplicated effort in the field.
- It demonstrates claim-discipline: calibration and uncertainty are reported before astrophysical interpretation.
- It adds a real-TTE bridge with robustness sweeps, showing portability checks and bounded conclusions.

## What The Community Gains
- A reusable benchmark scaffold for injection-recovery and surrogate-based significance testing.
- Public artifact trails for detector comparisons, calibration behavior, and decision gates.
- A transparent mixed/negative result that helps prevent overclaiming from underpowered detections.

## Scope-Locked Claim
- Current evidence supports a **methods/calibration contribution**, not observational QPO confirmation.
- Recommended publication posture: mixed/negative methods result with reproducible artifacts and explicit guardrails.
